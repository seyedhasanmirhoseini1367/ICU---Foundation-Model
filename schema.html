Most Important Files
★ Rank 1 — Pre-training signal
chartevents (vitals + 100k)
The raw time-series that a foundation model trains on. Every row is one measurement at one moment — heart rate, BP, SpO₂, temperature charted roughly every hour per patient. In the full dataset: 433 million rows across 94k ICU stays.

This is what you tokenise, mask, and predict during pre-training. Without it there is no foundation model.
433M rows in full file
★ Rank 2 — Outcome labels
admissions.csv
Contains every downstream evaluation label: hospital_expire_flag (in-hospital mortality), los_days (length of stay), discharge_location (home vs SNF vs died), admission_type (elective vs emergency).

Also holds race, insurance, language — essential for fairness audits, which reviewers now require for clinical AI papers.
The label table for every task
★ Rank 3 — Complementary signal
labevents_sample.csv
Lab results (creatinine, glucose, WBC, hemoglobin…) are less frequent than vitals — roughly once per day — but more clinically decisive. Sepsis, AKI, and organ failure are detected through labs first.

For multicentre work: lab tests are far more standardised across hospitals than charted vitals, making them better generalisation anchors when training across sites.
Best signal for cross-site transfer

icustays.csv — needed to link stay_id → hadm_id and to compute ICU LOS (a key target variable).  ·  diagnoses.csv — provides ICD codes for cohort selection (e.g. "all sepsis patients") and label stratification.  ·  patients.csv — demographics; small but required to compute patient age and join everything to a person.  

For the GPT-MEDIC PhD specifically: the core data pipeline is chartevents + labevents → tokenised event stream per patient, with admissions.hospital_expire_flag as the primary evaluation label.
